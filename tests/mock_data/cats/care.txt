Cats are obligate carnivores, meaning they require a diet high in animal protein to stay healthy.

Cats spend about 30-50% of their day grooming themselves, which helps regulate body temperature and reduce stress.

Cats have a unique way of showing affection through slow blinking, which is often called "cat kisses."

Cats need vertical space to climb and perch, as they feel safer when they can observe their environment from above.

Cats are crepuscular, meaning they are most active during dawn and dusk, which is when they naturally hunt.

Cats use their whiskers to navigate and measure spaces - they should never be trimmed as they're essential sensory tools.

Cats need regular dental care as they are prone to dental disease, which can lead to serious health problems.

Cats are territorial animals and need their own space, including separate food and water bowls and litter boxes.

Cats communicate through body language, vocalizations, and scent marking. Understanding their signals is key to their care.

Cats need mental stimulation through play, puzzle feeders, and environmental enrichment to prevent boredom.
