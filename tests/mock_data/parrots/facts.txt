Parrots are among the most intelligent bird species.
Some parrots can live up to 80 years in captivity.
Parrots have zygodactyl feet - two toes forward and two back.
The African Grey Parrot can learn over 1,000 words.
Parrots are found in tropical and subtropical regions worldwide.
Parrots are the only birds that can use their feet to bring food to their mouth.
The Kakapo is the world's only flightless parrot.
Parrots have a strong, curved beak for cracking nuts and seeds.
Parrots can see ultraviolet light, which humans cannot.
The Hyacinth Macaw is the largest parrot species.
