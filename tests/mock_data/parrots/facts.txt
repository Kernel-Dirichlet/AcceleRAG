Parrots are among the most intelligent birds, with some species having the intelligence of a 4-year-old child.

There are over 350 different species of parrots, ranging from tiny budgies to large macaws.

Parrots are the only birds that can use their feet to bring food to their mouths.

Some parrots can live for over 80 years, making them one of the longest-living bird species.

Parrots have zygodactyl feet, meaning they have two toes pointing forward and two pointing backward.

The African Grey Parrot can learn over 1,000 words and understand their meanings.

Parrots are found on every continent except Antarctica.

The Kakapo, a flightless parrot from New Zealand, is the world's heaviest parrot.

Parrots have a specialized tongue that helps them manipulate food and make sounds.

Some parrot species can fly at speeds up to 50 miles per hour.
