Dogs have been domesticated for over 15,000 years, making them one of the first domesticated animals.

The average dog can understand up to 250 words and gestures, similar to a 2-year-old child.

Dogs have three eyelids, including a nictitating membrane that helps protect and moisten the eye.

A dog's nose is wet to help absorb scent chemicals, enhancing their sense of smell.

Dogs can detect some diseases in humans, including certain types of cancer and low blood sugar.

The world's oldest dog lived to be 29 years old.

Dogs can see in color, but their color vision is limited compared to humans.

Dogs have about 1,700 taste buds, while humans have about 9,000.

Dogs can hear sounds at frequencies up to 65,000 Hz, while humans can only hear up to 20,000 Hz.

Dogs have been to space - the first living creature to orbit Earth was a dog named Laika.
